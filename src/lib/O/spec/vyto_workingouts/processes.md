## 1. The substrate law — every stage is C in, C out

The one bet is that everything is the same legible living matter.  Cash it here: **every
 intermediate of this machine is itself a C-tree.**  The mirror is C (tok-keyed rows).  The
  distillation is C (`%Vtuffing/%Vrow/%Vbit`).  Importance and the solved S ride C.  Envelopes,
   holds, moments, the board's own organ rows — C, C, C.  Two consequences that ARE the assurance:

- **Engines compose with no impedance mismatch.**  A pipeline stage is a function tree→tree;
   chaining them is composition, and a new engine slots in by matching the shape, never by
    inventing a bridge format.
- **Everything is provable the same one way.**  A C-tree snaps; a snap is a fixture; a fixture is
   a Book.  "Prove each station in isolation" isn't a habit that might not scale — it is
    guaranteed by the substrate.  Stuffing and Typescale already demonstrated it end-to-end.

## 3. The joints — where the asks meet, and why each holds

- **J1 · Local geometry, global price.**  The spec's scope recursion (§5: parent sees only the
   envelope) seemed to fight the sizing law's ONE global S (§9).  It doesn't: **demand sums UP
    the envelopes, one S comes DOWN** — geometry is solved locally per scope, but every text in
     every scope is priced by the same S, so size stays a graph-global sentence.  The circularity
      (what's visible depends on S; S depends on demand; demand depends on what's visible) is the
       exact fixed point Typescale already proved: fold-below-floor only ever *lowers* demand, so
        S rises monotonically within a pass — no oscillation; un-folding happens only across
         passes, under hysteresis.
- **J2 · The floor law** (HUMAN 1).  Text-fold, family-crush, and scope-freeze are one
   comparison: `S·φ(w)·ψ(dist) < floor → supersede by distillation`.  Crush flutter near the
    threshold is the old vanishing-node dance — and the spec already owns the cure: membership
     decisions ride the flight-latch (§3) and S glides with hysteresis.  The first %Situation to
      write: *membership flipped twice within one settle*.
- **J3 · The crush's voice is the distiller.**  What a crushed pane SAYS = `Stuff_distil` of its
   members: Gang decides *who represents*, Stuffing decides *what gets said*.  And the spec's
    %Bunch crest ("Artist written a single time across the group", §6) is the same algebra at the
     member level — the vein is one value across keys, the crest one value across members.  The
      engine is built and green; this joint is a call site, not new code.
- **J4 · Nest-solve.**  "A very visually identifiable shape to mean C, labels within, another /C
   inside" = the solver recursing into cell polygons: each scope solves its children inside its
    own polygon (power_cells + clip_halfplane — both already in `vyto_geometry.ts`), type-tab +
     name as the cell's crown, nucleus law intact.  The rectangular analog is numerically proven
      (the structure artifact, 35 cards, depth 4, zero layout violations:
       https://claude.ai/code/artifact/cdccea4b-020b-4238-8767-a1c140a0f79c — click-zoom is J5's
        sketch; the earlier rosette/vein demo:
         https://claude.ai/code/artifact/87eb6234-e2d7-495e-9d57-af1a57d73153).  Sliver polygons
          at depth are dodged by the shape catalogue — a deep scope may declare `slab` and get
           slice-and-dice inside its cell; the catalogue is the knob, not a hack.
- **J5 · Reframe** (HUMAN 2).  Surf = choose a new root scope; its polygon becomes the frame;
   ④–⑦ re-run on the subtree; S rises; exactly the newly-affordable set un-crushes.  Delivered
    as a Focus **shift** (collect → hold → choreograph → settle → moment), so the zoom explains
     itself and the uninvolved stay still.  There is no separate LOD system — **the floor law IS
      the LOD**, which is why zooming and distilling can never disagree.
