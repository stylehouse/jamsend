## 1. Why a catalogue

Engines (processes.md) say HOW the machine transforms; this doc says WHAT the recurring shapes of
 C** are and how the one language reads each.  The bet: C** has a small set of structural idioms —
  the same ones CLAUDE.md teaches — and if each has ONE saying, the whole glass becomes legible the
   way the snap is: you learn the notation once and read anything.

## 2. The structures of C** — and the one saying for each

1. **The holding** — a thing existing ONCE under its shelf as its mainkey (a `%Record` in
    `stock/`, materialising its `%Stream,seq` audio chunks — `Ra.g:631`, `Radiola.g:150`).
     *Saying:* a cell — type-tab colour + name within, children inside (Nestcut).  The mainkey
      IS the tab; identity is the cell.
2. **The referring particle** — names a thing elsewhere, wearing its OWN mainkey + the id
    (`Spin,of:X`).  *Saying:* a thread landing **nucleus-to-nucleus** (`Vyto_sizing §8`) — NEVER a
     second cell impersonating the holding.  The reference reads as a relation, not a thing.
3. **The 1:1 join** — `Card,id:X` beside `Record,id:X`, the shared id the join.  *Saying:* one
    pane with two PEER nuclei (§8's join-cell) — two identities, one room, the seam readable.
4. **The ledger** — many:1 event rows (`Spin,of:X` · `Like,of:X` · `Heist,at:pier`).  *Saying:*
    a COUNT worn by the target (the spread's counted-chip grammar — ×N) with the individual
     events behind a dip.  A thousand spins is one loud number and one door — never a thousand cells.
5. **The homogeneous family** — N siblings of one mainkey (the crush's home).  *Saying:* below
    the floor, the crest — `Stuff_distil`'s fact · spread · vein — plus the dip; above it, member
     cells.  Same sentence, two registers, zoom picks (Floorlaw).
6. **The spool / the ring** — ordered moments, drop-oldest, o-marks and blessings.  *Saying:*
    a **band** (the ordered shape of the catalogue) — order IS the meaning, so it never
     tessellates; it strips.
7. **The req tree** — transient scaffolding at maz levels, dropped when served.  *Saying:* mostly
    UNSAID — the machine's own work sits below the floor by default; a dip on the world row opens
     the in-flight few worth seeing.  (An owner drops its finished reqs; the glass echoes the law.)
8. **The scope** — a C whose children are a world of their own (`H > A > w`, a Book's bench, a
    commissioned glass).  *Saying:* containment — the child world solves INSIDE the parent's
     polygon (Nestcut), envelope out, one global S down (J1).
9. **The furniture** — `.c` runtime matter: holds, polys, backlinks, watchers.  *Saying:*
    normally NONE — only sc is said, `.c` invisible by default and that is the product aim.  But
     the human's framing (2026-07-22) is sharper than "a diagnostic exception": showing `.c` is
      **just another way to divide sc presentation space for coherence — properties truly sc vs
       those merely c.**  The language ALREADY divides the presentation space (facts here, spreads
        there, veins lifted, joint behind the dip); the sc-vs-c boundary is simply one more such
         division axis — a **data-dump register** draws the line so a reader can see which
          properties are real persisted matter and which are runtime furniture.  So it is NOT a
           second notation and NOT a forensics mode — it is the one language applying its own
            divide-for-coherence rule to the c/sc seam, hidden by default, drawn on request.  (The
             board's "holds" toggle is a coarse instance of the same division.)
10. **The crest itself** — a `%Vtuffing` is C like everything else, so the language can say a
     SAYING (distil a shelf of crests — the deep crest).  Compression recurses because the
      notation is made of its own matter.  **PROVEN 2026-07-22 — the `Deepcrest` Book**
       (Voronation.g): `Deep_memberise` reads a crest back into a plain `%Gist` member (veins
        re-enter as the facts they superseded — the read run one door deep; spreads refuse to
         collapse — a blur never fakes a voice) and the crest of crests agrees with the crest of
          the raw pool on every family-uniform key, while the top door carries and re-carries the
           true summed count (§4 Dip_assign, §6 injectivity — both exercised at height).

## 3. What the Se wants — provenance as the language's honesty

The `%Se` today (VoroTest's model harness) is phase provenance: small rows a station leaves on the
 model saying what IT did that pass.  In the one language every saying wants to answer **"why do
  you look like this"** without leaving the tree:

- **`by:`** — which engine said it (fold · gang · vein · scale · floor · relate).
- **`rule:`** — which law fired (floor-supersede · overflow-cap · vein-supersede ·
   universal-presence-skip · gang-eviction).
- **`hid:`** — what the saying subsumed: the count and the way back in (this is the dip's freight —
   §4).  A crest that says `genre dub` once owes the reader the five it speaks for.
- **`pass:`** — the beat/stir it was computed on, so staleness is readable and a Book can assert
   freshness.

The reader's guarantee that makes "accurately readable" TRUE: **nothing hidden is unreachable,
 and every hiding states its rule.**  Supersession without provenance is lying by omission; with
  it, compression is honest.

## 4. Dip_assign — no supersession is ever doorless

> **Every act of supersession ASSIGNS a dip.**  The crest wears the family's door; the spread's
>  `+N` tail IS a dip; a vein's subsumed per-key facts sit behind one; a crushed scope, a capped
>   ledger, a below-floor branch — each hiding mints its handle, stamped with the Se's `hid:` and
>    `rule:`.

**BUILT 2026-07-22 (the tenancy grant executed):** `Vyto_dip_assign` in Vyto.g — every crest the
 fold's re-homed distiller mints leads with `%Vrow,row:dip` carrying the true count + `.c.members`

   Books-invisible) mints.  Gated by the **VytoCrest** Book: the lying-door probe (count forced to

Two unifications fall out:

- **Dip = reframe's door.**  Surfing a dip and zooming into a scope are ONE gesture (J5): both

- **A dip is a DOOR, not a cache** — the bounded-memory rider the human attached to the reframe
   ruling (§5).  Nothing behind a dip is retained solved; entering re-derives.

## 6. The read — what "accurately readable" MEANS, and how to prove it (the big think, 2026-07-22)

*(Written on Opus at the human's ask for "one more big think" before the overnight grind resumes on
 Fable.  This is the keystone: every engine so far WRITES the glass; this section is the one that
  says the glass can be READ, and turns the phrase "pretty accurately readable C** language" from a
   wish into a contract with a test.)*

### 6.1 The contract

A notation is a language only if it has an inverse.  The snap has one — `deL` decodes what `enL`
 encoded, exactly.  The glass must earn the same claim, so state it as a function:

>   **`read(saying) → C**`**, and the law: **`read(render(tree)) ≡ tree`, modulo declared dips.**

"Modulo declared dips" is the whole subtlety, and it is not a weakening — it is the definition of
 what compression is *allowed* to do.  Unpacked into two separate guarantees:

- **Accuracy = it round-trips.**  Rendering then reading returns the original tree, EXCEPT that a
   subtree behind a dip comes back as its **dip-stub** (mainkey + `hid:` count + the way in), not
    its expanded contents.  And — this is why Dip_assign is load-bearing, not decoration — the
     dip-stub *is in the tree* (§4).  So nothing is lost; hidden matter is **relocated behind a
      readable door**, and `read` recovers the door with its exact count.  A supersession that did
       not mint a countable dip would break the round-trip: that is the formal reason doorless
        hiding is banned.
- **Readability = the read is unambiguous (injective).**  Two DIFFERENT trees must never render to
   the same saying with nothing to tell them apart.  This is the sharper, easier-to-violate half,
    and it is where the grammar earns its keep (6.2).

### 6.2 Where readings could collide — and the exact feature that separates them

Injectivity is not free; each near-collision names a feature that must exist for the language to
 stay honest.  Walking the ambiguities IS the derivation of the grammar:

- **A fact said once vs a lone particle.**  A crest saying `genre dub` for a family of five, and a
   single holding whose `genre` is `dub`, say the same *words*.  The distinguisher is **cardinality
    carried on the container**: the family is a cell wearing a dip of `hid:5`; the lone holding is a
     bare cell.  → Injectivity is why `Dip_assign` must carry a **count**, not just a door.
- **The marginal vs the joint — the honest boundary.**  A spread `year: 2007(2) 1998(1)` round-trips
   to the *multiset* of years, but NOT to which title carried which year — the cross-key pairing is
    behind the member dip.  So the compressed register preserves **per-key marginals + counts**, and
     the joint distribution lives behind the door.  This is the precise, defensible statement of what
      the crest keeps, and it explains the **vein** exactly: the vein is the ONE cross-key correlation
       lifted out of the behind-the-dip joint and promoted to a first-class marginal (a value across
        keys).  Veins are how you pull the specific joint facts worth keeping up into the readable
         layer; everything else stays legitimately behind the dip.  "Say more with less" is precise
          here: less = the joint is dipped; more = the marginals and the chosen veins are said.
- **Two registers of the same tree.**  Crest and cells are not two encodings that might disagree —
   they are `read` **stopped at different door-depths** of ONE tree.  The crest is `read` that halts
    at the family dip; the cells are `read` that has surfed one dip deeper.  Because both are partial
     reads of the same C**, they are automatically consistent — which is the formal content of the
      human's steer *"registers not modes."*  A mode is a different renderer; a register is the same
       reader at a different depth.  Modes can contradict; registers cannot.

### 6.3 Why the Se is the grammar, not garnish

`read` is a parser, and a parser needs to know which production fired.  That is exactly the Se's job
 (§3): `by:`/`rule:` tell the reader which engine and which law produced a row, so `read` inverts the
  right rule; `hid:` gives it the count and the door to recurse through; `pass:` lets a reader reject
   a stale saying instead of mis-decoding it.  So the Se feature list the human asked about is not
    telemetry bolted on — **it is the set of annotations that make the notation decodable at all.**
     A row without its `rule:` is a word whose part of speech you have to guess; the language stops
      being "accurately readable" the moment a saying can't say how it was made.  This is the
       strongest argument for building the Se features early: they are load-bearing for the read,
        and the read is the product.

### 6.4 The proof — a Readback Book (queued, ruling-free)

The round-trip is testable with no glass, the way Stuffing tested distil: author a bench C**, run
 `Stuff_distil` to the crest, then a NEW `Stuff_read` that inverts the crest back to a **recovered
  tree**, and assert:

- recovered per-key marginals ≡ original marginals (accuracy on what the register keeps);
- every dip's `hid:` count equals the true subsumed count (the injectivity feature);
- surfing the member dip (feeding the behind-the-door members) recovers the full original set
   (nothing lost — relocated);
- two DIFFERENT benches that share a marginal but differ in the joint produce **distinguishable**
   sayings (the adversarial half: a probe that drops the count must let them collide → red).

That Book — call it **Readback** — is the capstone proof: it demonstrates the glass is a
 lossless-with-declared-doors notation, which is the literal meaning of "accurately readable."  It
  is added to tonight's queue (item 4) ahead of the pods variant, because it proves the north star
   rather than decorating it.

**PROVEN 2026-07-22 — the Book is green ×4 + red-gated** (Voronation.g: `Read_crest` inverts ·
 `Read_audit` judges claimed-vs-truth as %Audit DATA · `Read_surf` opens the dip · `Read_saying`
  normalises a crest for twin comparison).  All four §6.4 assertions hold, and the audit did its
   deeper job — it MEASURED the notation and found **two leaks**, both said as %see data:
- **A valued fact carries no carrier count.**  Three-of-five members carry `room:attic` → the
   crest says `room: attic` bare → the read must believe all five.  (The '1'-presence leg already
    counts — `n:have` — the valued leg simply never got the same honesty.)
- **A spread tail counts dropped VALUES not carriers.**  Six cities over eleven members → chips
   keep seven carriers · the `+3` tail hides FOUR members with no way to know.
   **HUMAN ruling owed (morning):** stamp `n:have` on valued facts and spreads in `Stuff_keyrows`
    (and mirror in the Voro.g oracle)?  It closes both leaks and makes the round trip exact
     everywhere — but it re-snaps three green Books (Stuffing · Floorlaw · Deepcrest) and touches
      the crush's live voice, so it is a preen-hour decision, not a night move.
Also proven en route: the twins — two families sharing every marginal but differing in the joint
 wear **byte-identical sayings** (all rankings count-decided; a first-seen tie would have leaked
  the joint into vein-key order — caught and defused in the bench), told apart ONLY through the
   counted door.  The probe was the contract's own: strip the vein-bit count from the read and the
    round trip collapses red.

### 6.5 What this reframes

Once the read is the contract, three things that looked like taste become correctness:

- **Doorless hiding is a bug**, not a style choice — it breaks the round-trip (6.1).
- **The vein is necessary**, not a nicety — without it the only cross-key correlations are behind
   the dip, and some are worth reading (6.2).
- **The Se must ship with the engines**, not after — it is the grammar the read parses (6.3).

And the write-fan (the parked ruling) gets a cleaner definition too: it is `read`'s door used for
 *writing* — you surf to a crest row, edit it, and the edit propagates to exactly the members the
  `hid:` door points at.  Editing through the readable summary is only safe *because* the summary
   round-trips; the write-fan is the read contract run in reverse.  Another reason the read is the
    keystone: it is what makes the most interesting manipulation engine even definable.
