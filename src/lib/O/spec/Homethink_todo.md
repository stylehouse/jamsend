# Homethink

The toplevel posture: what the whole thing IS, said once, so a fork inherits the WHY and not
 just the mechanics. CLAUDE.md carries the mechanics; the spec corpus carries the details;
  this carries the unity. The register is internal — the codebase as a community of ghosts
   talking to itself — because that is the community this has to resonate with first.

## 0. What to get on with next

This doc is mostly read, not worked: a fresh session reads §1–§4 to stand somewhere before
 touching anything. Its own open work:
- Say the bet SHORTER. The test: a stranger reads §1 and can predict what a new subsystem
   here would look like before being shown one.
- When a design fight breaks out and this doc can't arbitrate it, the doc is missing a
   clause — come back and add the clause, don't just win the fight.
- Candidate `_spec`, not self-promoted: the human preens it into shape when it's earned.

## 1. The one bet

**Turn every kind of state — a song, a test, a friendship, an error — into the same
 legible living matter, held where a group can see it, prove it, and rewrite it while it
  runs. Then pour music through it first.**

Every subsystem is that sentence at a different altitude:
- *legible* — everything serialises to a snap: state is prose you can read and a diff you
   can trust. If it matters, it snaps.
- *living* — the matter is reactive all the way down, and the machine rewrites itself
   through its own matter: a dock compiles into the very ghost that runs it, live, no
    reboot.
- *matter* — ONE substrate. A particle's type is its mainkey and nothing else; there is no
   second substrate hiding in a class hierarchy or an ad-hoc store.
- *a group* — identity is a key, not a name. Grants and revocation tombstones are
   decision-facts in the matter; nothing routes or trusts on a nickname.
- *prove* — a claim about the machine is believed only when a LIVE runner said so, in a
   sentence a human would say, latched where its absence complains by name.
- *music first* — Radiobuddies is the main spring. Magazines, husks, cursors and the
   standing publish are what the edifice is FOR: a few people holding a music collection
    in common.

## 2. The community of ghosts

Each ghost keeps one station; the unity is that no station duplicates another's.

- **Stuff** is the ground — TheC, `sc`|`c`, `o`|`i`|`oai`, Travel. Everything anyone owns
   is made of the same clay, so any ghost can hold any other's things.
- **Housing** is the town — `H:` districts, `w:` workshops, `A:` actors; `beliefs()` is
   the town clock, and the mutex means one speaker at a time, so a reader always sees a
    frozen town.
- **Hovercraft** is how work asks for time — transient `%req` trees queue at maz levels
   and bow out on a ttlilt; a req is for TREES of transient moves, and work that should be
    plain sync stays plain sync.
- **Text** is the tongue — anything can be said aloud (`enWaft`) and heard back without
   loss; a protocol only decides what politeness strips (`omit_sc`).
- **Story** is the sworn record — a Book testifies in steps; `%see` observes, `%seen`
   swears (latched + rostered in `The/Assertions`, so a regression is a NAMED complaint);
    the snap-fixture diff is the court of record, and only a live runner's word is
     admissible.
- **Cyto | Matstyle | Voro** are the face — the town seen live, styled by kind rather
   than by hand; Voro is the town squinting at itself until structure becomes a `%Seem`.
- **Lies** is the renovation guild — editable docks compile into running ghosts while the
   town stays inhabited; the editor and the runners are rooms of one house, not tools
    beside it.
- **Cluster | Swarm | Keep | Peeroleum** are the diplomacy — how two Houses hold the same
   matter: signed identity, grants and tombstones, replication that PULLS.
- **Musu | Radio | Repli** are the purpose — the music: censuses sublime into magazines,
   husks keep the wire cheap, cursors survive renames because facts heal them.

## 3. What counts as true here

- A green that didn't come from a live runner is a rumor — the headless boot quiesces at
   its own depth and its fixtures match only itself.
- An assertion is a sentence a human believes, and it must be believable STRUCTURALLY:
   `%see` observes (per-beat, drops — the drop is signal), `%seen` swears (latched +
    rostered — a missing declared sentence reds the run even at 100% steps), `%log` merely
     shows. The Seen_split exists because `%see` was carrying oaths it could drop without
      anyone noticing.
- The snap-diff stays the ocean: the place to notice what nobody thought to assert.
- A test must be able to FAIL — sabotage it once so the wire is proven load-bearing.

## 4. Tells that the bet is being broken

- State a group would need, hoarded in `.c` or a closure — hidden matter.
- A green that skipped the live runner — rumor.
- An assertion sentence no human would say — noise wearing a badge.
- Routing or trusting on a nickname — vanity.
- A one-off script where a Book should be — a stranger doing the town's work.
- A contract implemented for SOME backends — a door only some may use.
- New bespoke machinery where a deletion was available — the measure of a good change is
   how much bespoke machinery it removes (Story_future's doctrine, adopted whole).
