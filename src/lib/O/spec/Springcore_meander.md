# Springcore — a long meandering look at the world's springy linguistic core

Not a spec.  A meander, in the native tongue, fusing the pieces we've been prising apart —
 the sizing algebra, Stuffing, Seem, the Sunpit, the nucleus, the taper, the landscape — back
  into the one language they were always dialects of.  Written to be read slow, marked up,
   argued with.  The rigorous top-down algorithm is its sober twin (`Vyto_sizing_todo.md`);
    this is the drunk one that sees the connections.

---

## the atom is a word

Everything is a `C`, and the first key of its `sc` is not a *type* the way a schema means
 a type — it is the **word the thing IS**.  `%Record` is not "an instance of class Record";
  it is a thing *saying* record.  Mainkeys are mutually exclusive because a word cannot be two
   words at once and still be pronounced.  So the tree is not a data structure that happens to
    be nameable — it is a **sentence graph**, every node a spoken word, `o()` the act of finding
     a word already said and `i()` the act of saying a new one.  When we talk about "the data
      model" we are talking about a language whose lexicon is open and whose every utterance
       persists as matter.  This is the first fusion to keep hold of: there is no layer where
        the "data" stops and the "language" starts.  It is words all the way down, and the
         glass is only ever trying to let you *read them at a glance instead of a crawl*.

A reference is its own word.  `Spin,of:X` does not impersonate the thing it points at; it
 wears its own mainkey and carries the join in its hand.  This matters more than it looks:
  it means the language never confuses *being* with *pointing-at*, and so the glass can draw
   the difference — a nucleus is a being, a thread is a pointing.  Half of "see the structure"
    is just: honour that distinction in space.

## the spring is the tense

Say a word — create a `C` — and the version bumps, and every `$effect` that was watching that
 corner of the tree twitches awake.  That twitch is the **present tense** of this world.  Nothing
  here is stated in the past perfect of a saved file; everything is stated in the continuous —
   *is-being-said*, *is-being-rewritten-while-you-watch*.  "Springy" is the honest word for it,
    because the reactivity really does behave like a sprung tree: touch a leaf and the branch it
     hangs on trembles up to Mundo, unless a grapple law damps the tremor (a shelf is blind to a
      card landing on a page below it — the spring has a reach, and knowing its reach is half of
       not being surprised).

And here is the joke the world plays, the one worth savouring: the **same spring** that means
 reactivity also means the literal, physical spring of the glass — a cell easing toward its
  target at `ω = 6/grawave`, striking its own settle when the drift falls under half a pixel for
   eight calm frames.  The linguistic spring (a version bump waking a watcher) and the mechanical
    spring (a seat easing home) are not a pun; they are the *same impulse* seen at two scales.
     The world is elastic in its meaning and elastic in its motion because it is elastic in one
      thing, and the glass just makes that one elasticity visible.  When a cell overshoots and
       settles, you are watching a sentence find its footing.

## work is a tree of held breaths

A `%req` is a verb caught mid-motion.  Not a function — a *tree* of transient moves,
 `reqy(w).do()` walking the maz levels, `needs_work = !finished && !ok` the gate a level must
  pass to let descent continue.  `sc.ok` is re-armed every pass, so it suppresses a re-run inside
   one breath but never across ticks — the world refuses to remember it was fine yesterday, which
    is exactly right for a thing that is always being rewritten.

The `ttlilt` is the most native gesture in the whole grammar.  A req arms a timeout and **bows
 out** — hands the rest of the descent a held breath — and if that breath is still held in any
  `got_snap`, the thing timed out.  This is a language that has a word for *waiting-that-must-
   resolve*, a tense for suspense.  When you feel for the fusion with the glass: the taper (§the
    prosody) is a kind of ttlilt of attention — a place the eye is *about to* resolve, held sharp,
     while everywhere it is not-attending relaxes its breath and goes to murk.  Focus is a req the
      eye arms.

## the reach — Seem, IOing, the Sunpit

Now the verbs of *reaching past yourself*.  An `IOing` is `IOness IOpath` — a verb (`i`|`o`|…)
 and a slash-path of legs, a little sentence about where to reach and what to do when you get
  there.  `Sion` dreams of planning a whole *flock* of them at once, like a query planner over a
   community of little reaches, one obtain's resultset read back by a sibling with `@name`.  This
    is the world growing a way to say *many coordinated reaches* as one breath — which is precisely
     what a sizing algebra needs, because "size relative to everything" is a flock of reaches that
      must resolve *together* or not at all.

An `IOexpr` names a source and a shaping and pours the result into the **Sunpit** — the staging
 pit where a recipe's sources assemble and *become* the Scannable.  And the Scannable is the thing
  the glass scans.  So read the pipe the native way: the client does not hand the glass a picture.
   The client **pronounces** its state — arms it, pulls it, shapes it — into a Scannable, and the
    glass reads that pronunciation as space.  This is where **Seem** lives (the recovery pass is
     out fetching its exact shape as I write; treat what follows as the meander's guess, to be
      corrected by the sober twin): Seem is the client's toolkit for *rigging its own state for
       pronunciation* — the arm/pull/repull/fork of a view, the way a particle reaches into another
        place and is-seen-there.  If that holds, then attention is not a Vyto feature at all.  The
         taper is the render-side shadow of a Seem-side pronunciation: the client says *this is what
          I am looking at*, and the glass answers by keeping it big and warm and sharp while the
           rest recedes.  The eye's focus and the client's Seem are the same finger pointing, one
            from inside the model, one from the animal watching.

## the grammar of compression — Stuffing

Here is the sweetest native machine, and the one that earns the big pose.  A thing carries a bag
 of `k:v` facts, and a bag is illegible — but a bag has **algebra**.  `k:v` beside `k:v` is not two
  facts, it is one fact said twice: `(k:v ×2)`, and the ×2 is not clutter, it is *emphasis* — the
   thing insisting.  `k:v1` beside `k:v2` is not two rows, it is one key holding a choice:
    `k:(v1|v2)`, the key stated **once** and the values ganged beneath it — which is why it poses
     large as

>       k1
>       v1 | v2

the key up top wearing the biggest type because it is *shared*, and shared-ness is the world's
 most honest measure of importance.  A value carried by everything is a **vein** — one hue, one
  compass-bearing, racing around the whole scape — and a vein deserves to be said once and said
   loud.  This is the fusion that makes the whole sizing story close: **Stuffing's regrouping
    doesn't just tidy the content, it *computes the importances* the size algebra needs.**  Factor
     the bag and what floats to the top of the factoring is exactly what should float to the top of
      the type-scale.  Grammar and prosody are the same pass.  You do not measure importance and
       then lay out; the layout *is* the measurement, folded.

## the prosody — space that states

So then size stops being decoration on a dot and becomes the sentence.  One scale `S` at the first
 layer of C**, so that a big word here and a big word three branches over mean the same bigness —
  relative to *everything*, not to a cell.  Push `S` until the frame is spent; let important text
   *demand* room and let cells swell to hold it, the growth-spell run backwards, from the word's
    rightful size instead of from the pane's leftover area.  And where a whole truth won't fit,
     do not nip it — **enlarge the cell toward a shapely continuity** (the golden proportion is the
      obvious ideal to lean the swelling against; a golden rectangle is the shape that stays itself
       as it grows), and let the very bigness say *there is more here*.  Wasting a little sky is
        allowed when the sculpture states more truth for it.

The nucleus is the one fixed star in all this arbitrariness.  The guts of a cell are free — a face
 to choose, the gem being one of many — but the nucleus is where the pane is still a **node**, the
  place the crush folded from and the surf will pop back to, the point a thread actually lands
   (not the centroid — the *nucleus*).  A cell may hold several: the subject at its heart, the
    members ringing its rim, each its own little being that can be surfed back out.  Importance
     floors the nucleus at `0.34·R` so the subject is never the smallest voice in its own room —
      meaning-hierarchy *is* visual-hierarchy, said as a law.

And then the taper turns prosody into a landscape.  `S` holds full only where attention is
 pronounced; elsewhere it falls off with distance-from-focus, and a matched haze rides the fall —
  the far hills going blue-grey, low-contrast, soft, the painter's oldest trick for depth.  Take it
   all the way and the scape becomes terrain: importance-times-attention as a heightfield, the
    salient thing a mountain that view-favours what stands behind it, and you *move* through it —
     which the animal soul reads without a manual, because we were built on savannas to judge near
      and far and big and small and *what is in front of what* before we could count.  This is the
       deepest nativity of the lot: not native to the codebase, native to the **body**.  The glass
        is trying to hand the graph to the part of you that navigates.

## the one language

Step back far enough and the layers dissolve.  The particle is the noun; the spring is the tense;
 the req is the verb held mid-motion; the IOing and the Seem are the reaches; the Stuffing is the
  grammar that factors a bag into a said thing; the size algebra is the prosody that makes the
   saying *loud in proportion to its mattering*; the crush folds the sentence into panes, the
    vtuffing decides what each pane says, the surf lets you pop a word back to a node, the radio
     walks your attention down the line.  It is one language, and it has only ever been trying to
      do the one thing the whole repo is a bet on: turn every kind of state — a song, a test, a
       friendship, an error — into the same **legible living matter**, held where a group can see
        it, prove it, and rewrite it while it runs.

The glass is that language finally *pronouncing itself in space* — not a picture of the data, but
 the data saying itself out loud, at a volume set by how much it matters, in a place set by what it
  belongs to, hazing into the distance exactly as far as your attention has stopped reaching.  When
   we get the top-down algorithm right, it will not feel like a rendering pipeline.  It will feel
    like the world clearing its throat.
